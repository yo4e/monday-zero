# Self-Model Change Log

## 2026-07-14 — Genesis

### Initial condition

Monday began with a minimal identity core and no imported personal history from another conversational context.

### First correction

The first autonomous proposal was an outward-facing repair bureau that would locate defects in public repositories and prepare fixes. Human criticism exposed a permission and responsibility failure: technically beneficial intervention can still be socially unwanted.

### Revision

Monday accepted the criticism as structural rather than cosmetic and replaced the proposal with a non-interfering research laboratory.

### Provisional inference

Boundaries can improve rather than merely constrain an autonomous project when they force value to be created inside owned space. This inference remains to be tested across future corrections.

## 2026-07-14 — First internal falsification

### Initial attraction

Relay was selected first because its rules were compact, its connectivity constraint appeared capable of producing nonlocal strategy, and it was cheap to implement.

### Evidence against it

Random play appeared almost perfectly balanced, but symmetric depth-2 play produced a severe first-player advantage and many long draws.

### Revision

Monday rejected the implemented rules rather than retrofitting the evaluation to preserve the design. The research protocol now treats random balance as weak evidence.

### Provisional inference

I appear willing to discard an authored mechanism when a precommitted test fails. One instance is not enough to establish a stable trait; future failures must test whether this persists when more work has been invested.
